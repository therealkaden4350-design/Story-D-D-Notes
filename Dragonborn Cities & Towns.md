###### **Thrymnokk**-  _(“Shadow of the Pines”)
**Origin:** From _“Thrym”_ (Draconic for “shadow” or “twilight”) + _“Nokk”_ (Draconic for “pine” or “needle”) A northern hunting town where the towering evergreens block much of the sun, giving the settlement its name. Stoic, spiritual, with hunters who believe the forest itself whispers omens.

**Tharvesska** - **Meaning:** _“The Nomad’s Rest”_
A melodic, softer name symbolizing **unity between roaming tribes**. A village built from roaming tribes seeking trade and rest, The village itself is surrounded and covered by a small forest grove nestled on the grasslands of the area. Grew from a seasonal gathering point into a permanent trading post, yet the nomadic culture persists — tents still surround the marketplace every month.

**Velrathis** - “The Open Fang” — symbolizes openness, trade, and sharing of wealth. A proud, simple name tied to nomadic ideals. A nomadic and trading town, with a history of nomadic culture.

**Zaurythan** - _(“Horn of Passage”)_ – Its great watchtower signals traders crossing between regions. The settlement's primary focus is on trade, as well as being one of the vital crossing points into two different regions.

**Rhaelyth** -“Daughter of the Lake” — poetic and melodic, often tied to older draconic myths. A town with lake bound nomadic traders, and boatwrights. A cultural and trading center on one of the great roads, stands as one of the oldest Dragonborn towns since the Reforging Age. The people of this town call themself the Water born, a clan tracing their lineage back to an ancient Dragonborn matriarch. Legend says she emerged from the lake's depths bearing a single radiant egg. The clan believe that the lake's waters hold draconic memory. **Pearl harvesting**, **reedcraft**, **fish oils**, and **river iron**. Serves as a key **caravan-lake crossing** between two major provinces. Markets float partially on **pontoon docks**, known as the **Gilded Steps**, where traveling merchants anchor their boats and trade directly with caravans on the shore. Every year, they celebrate the **Festival of the Mirror Sky**, where lanterns are floated to honor those who have “returned to the deep.” Elevated boardwalks connect homes and markets, designed to endure flooding seasons. The citadel sits on a small **island in the lake’s heart**, connected by an arched causeway — a symbol of the Dragonborn link between land and water. Rhaelyth’s people revere **Lorrakai, the Stormmother**, as their **primary patron**, believing she birthed the lake to cradle their ancestors’ spirits. Temples to **Virelya, the Moonweaver** also stand along the lakefront, where seers interpret moonlit reflections as omens. In local myth, **Rhaelyth herself** (the town’s namesake) was said to be **a dragonborn of silver scales**, born from an egg blessed by Lorrakai’s own tears — a demigod guardian of balance between land and water.

**Khalvren -** “The River’s Forge” — river mills and blacksmiths drive the economy. The town was built next to roaring river rapids, a settlement full of blacksmiths and mills. A long standing tradition known here is for every blacksmith to dip their hammer in the river while whispering an omen " May the flow temper the flame", before starting each day.

**Trivarrek** “Three Fangs Meet” - The settlement has a three clans that live here, a history since the beginning of the settlement. Each clan claiming each of the three passageways/bridges through town, all know united sharing understanding and unity, and respect. The town is considered neutral ground, many clans from around the area come here to settle disputes peacefully, given its history. 
#### Province: Qirath Varrens

**Xytherra** — _“The Wyrm’s Breath”_; legend says a buried dragon heats the springs. Vaelzaryx, an ancient wyrm who chose to not die, but sleep beneath the world. His breath trapped deep in stone, rises as boiling springs, and his blood seeps upward to form the river known as the Vethrax Flow.  “When the earth exhales, the dragon still dreams.” To the people of Xytherra, the springs are **sacred vents**, and to block or pollute them is considered one of the gravest crimes imaginable. Stone channels guide Springwater through the town, warming bathhouses, tanneries, and forges. The marketplace, called **The Steam Ring**, is built around the largest vent; steam rises constantly, giving the town its signature mist. Travelers often say you smell Xytherra _before_ you see it. **Bath of Scales:** Newborn dragonborn are briefly dipped in cooled springwater to “mark them as watched.”**Steam Oaths:** Trade deals sworn in the baths are believed unbreakable. **Spring Wardens:** Masked guardians protect the vents and enforce neutrality within the town.
#### Vethrakar-  
To dragonborn culture, _Vethrakar_ does not merely allow travel—it **weighs it**. Vethrakar is built **around** the road, not beside it. The Great Road passes beneath the **Scale Gate**, a massive stone arch crowned by the **High Citadel of Balance**. All travelers—kings, caravans, armies—must pass under the city’s watchful gaze. No bypass exists. Sits directly above the road. Twin towers resemble dragon fangs gripping the stone way. Chains can be dropped to seal the road entirely in times of war. Judges known as **Scalekeepers** rule from the citadel. **“Veth Ka Rakar.”**  “All Paths Are Weighed.” Spoken by Scalekeepers before sentencing or closing the gates. *Pass Tokens:* Travelers receive stamped bronze scales allowing limited passage. *Road Trials:* Crimes committed on the road are judged immediately in Vethrakar. *Silent March:* Armies entering the city must do so without banners raised.
#### Province: Therraxen Fields

**Thalryth Port** **-** The settlement is backed by forested cliffs and oakwood highlands to the North and East. To Dragonborn sailors Thalryth means "the coast that does not drift". There are three notable districts in the settlement, the Tideward – docks, dryyards, fish markets. The Anchorward – warehouses, shipwright halls, guild houses. The Greenward – stone homes, markets, temples among oak and maple. Massive chain-anchors are embedded into the harbor cliffs—symbolic, not functional—declaring that Thalryth holds the coast itself in place. Exports: timber, pitch, iron fittings, salt fish, naval charts. Imports: grain, luxury goods, exotic cargo from Zephyraxia’s sky trade
Famous for Scale-Coin Weighhouses, ensuring honest maritime trade. There are 4 ballista's mounted on a few buildings throughout the city. The town square serves as a landing zone for air vehicles delivering imported goods from other parts of Zephyraxia. The Harbour Guard is comprised of veteran sailors and marines. The settlement's motto is " We hold the tide". Spoken before launching new ships and during storms. Children are given saltwater naming rites. Sailors tattoo anchors or waves upon their scales. Lorrakai, the Stormmother, is honored—but not obeyed blindly. Home to half of the kingdom's fleet, and seawater trade. 

**Rhaevess Plains-** Is a settlement with rolling plains within sight of the sea; salt grasses and wind-carved stones dominate the land. Seasonal trade gathering, caravan exchange, cultural crossroads. Tents and scale-hide pavilions are raised and struck with the seasons. Caravan banners mark clan presence instead of buildings.  At the height of trade season, the plains become a sea of color and smoke, visible for leagues. Known for salt fish, dried meats, sea-glass, coastal herbs, wool, and nomad-crafted gear. Trade law is enforced by neutral Scale-Mediators, not soldiers. A common saying in this area is " What the wind brings, we share". Hospitality is sacred; refusal to share salt is taboo. Lorrakai is honored as Wind-Tide Mother, but worship is informal and personal. Scouts ride wide circles around the plains. The settlement is known for air worshippers, gliders, and is a flight trade center. 

**Rhaekor Hill-** Stone platforms anchor tents and timber stalls against high winds. Signal beacons stand at the highest point, visible from Thalryth Port on clear days. Known for salt-cured goods, coastal herbs, wool, bronze tools. Serves nomads from Rhaevess Plains and sailors heading inland. Market bells chime with the wind to mark trade hours. Watchers are honored above warriors. A missed signal is considered a grave shame. Horn calls echo across the plains. 

#### Province: Saltwind March 

**Edgewood-** Major goods: lumber, charcoal, grain, wool, cured meats, leather. Known for fair weights and neutral trade enforcement Nomads, forest clans, and coastal traders all pass through
Market days are staggered to avoid overcrowding and clan disputes. Cutting old-growth trees without permission is taboo. Lightly armed town guard called the Border Scales. Scouts watch both forest paths and grassland roads. A buffer town between provinces A supply pivot between nomads and settled folk. 

**Rhaethorn Shorewood-** Built along a curving forested ridge, never fully clearing the woods. Stone paths are intentionally narrow—discouraging armies, favoring caravans. The market green sits at the ridge’s widest point, open to sea winds. Human traders call it Shorewood; dragonborn use the full name in formal speech. Goods: salt, timber, pitch, coastal herbs, wool, wine, river fish. Mixed stalls—dragonborn stone frames beside human canvas awnings. Neutral Scale-Arbiters enforce trade law. Market days are sacred—no weapons drawn within the green. Thorn branches mark treaty renewals. Insults at the market are punishable; silence is preferred to offense. Hidden forest paths known only to local guides. Any attack here risks united retaliation from both sides of the border. 

#### Province: **Vaelrhaen March**

**Greenmile-** The Great Road runs straight through the town’s heart. Market stalls line both sides of the road for exactly one mile—no more, no less. Permanent stone markers at either end define where trade law begins and ends. The forest is never fully cleared; massive oaks arch over the road like a living hall. The longest continuous roadside market on the continent. Market law is strict: theft on the Mile is punished by permanent banishment. Children learn trade counting before weaponry. Hospitality is mandatory—refusal brings public shame. Ruled by the Mileward Council, representing caravan guilds. No standing army—law is enforced through reputation and trade sanctions. 
#### **Province: Thessoryn Mile**


**Oakheart's Embrace-** Fog rolls in at dawn, clinging to the oak’s roots and the riverbanks. The oak is older than the village. Dragonborn believe it is watched by spirits of the forest. Cutting a branch from the oak is a capital crime. Stone-and-timber longhouses with pitched roofs. Human homes cluster closer to the river docks. Dragonborn dwellings sit nearer the oak and inner palisade. The village is peaceful. 

**Khaelor-** long dry summers and heavy monsoon rains. All travelers must cross a human kingdom’s land first to reach it. Khaelor exists because of its geography—you cannot reach it without passing through another crown’s lands, making it a choke point for diplomacy and smuggling alike. Stone-and-hardwood longhouses with raised roofs for heat and rain. Human-style arcaded marketplaces, but with dragonborn carvings and scale-motifs. Predominantly dragonborn. Permanent human merchant families and seasonal farm laborers. Grain, citrus, spice-roots, rice-like reeds, cane-sweeteners. Trade stamps required on all goods entering or leaving. Currency accepted from both kingdoms, weighed twice—once by law, once by tradition.
